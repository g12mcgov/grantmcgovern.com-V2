I have had the privilege of being taught by great mentors along my path of becoming a software developer. I have met incredible people through internships, and learned amazing things.

My first love is Python, but at school I tend to write strictly in C++. I sometimes find myself writing HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, but I prefer a backend approach. I am a Mac OS X evangelist and really anything Unix of the sort. I believe in utterly simply designs and sheer minimalism. When I'm not bossing computers around, you can find me reposting new house-music tunes on SoundCloud, building things out of wood, or here.

In my spare time, I hack code for 1834 Software, Wake Forest University's student run software development firm.
